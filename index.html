I am working as a scientist in Foundations of Computing group at TRDDC, one of the research-and-innovation labs of Tata Consultancy Services Ltd, located in Pune, India. My research interests span across several aspects of static analysis/program verification and software engineering. I have been exploring postprocessing of alarms as a mean to address the problem of static analysis alarms. More particularly, I aim to improve precision and performance of static analysis tools while maintaining their scalability on very large software systems, and to improve and support manual inspection of the results of static analysis tools. Currently, I am also exploring ways to benefit from the advances in machine learning to improve static/program analysis and postprocessing of alarms. In a more or less distant past I also used to work on identification and implementation of verification techniques to detect of new types of defects, automated test data generation, and structuring and composing of software development artifacts in software product line engineering.

Since 2016 I have also been pursuing my PhD degree under supervision of Dr. Alexander Serebrenik and Prof. Dr. M.G.J. van den Brand, both from Eindhoven University of Technology (TU/e), The Netherlands. Addressing same problem of alarms also has been the topic my PhD: Postprocessing of Static Analysis Alarms . More details about my PhD and thesis can be found here. During this period, I got opportunity to visit TU/e five times, each visit being for the duration of around a month, and have Dr. Alexander Serebrenik's sabbatical at TRDDC twice, each sabbatical visit spanning over a couple of weeks.

At TRDDC, prior to working as scientist/researcher, I worked on designing a technique for structuring and composing of software development artifacts in software product line engineering (2007 to 2009), and then on developing tools to automatically generate test data for different coverage criteria (2009-2010). Between 2011 and 2013, I was involved in design and development of a commercial static analysis tool tool, TCS Embedded Code Analyzer (TCS ECA). I contributed by designing and implementation of techniques for detecting defects of unique types, and techniques for improving precision of underlying analyses such as value and pointer analysis. Based on the research work at TRDDC, I have (co)-authored 15 peer-reviewed conference papers, and filed 12 unique patents of which eight are granted in one or more jurisdictions and the other four are currently under examination.

I obtained my Bachelor of Engineering degree in Computer Science in 2006 at Maharashtra Institute of Technology, affiliated to University of Pune, India. After obtaining the Bachelor's degree in June 2006 and prior to joining TRDDC (TCS in 2007), I worked with IBM India as a developer.
